Arkaprava Choudhury

I prefer slow over-the-board chess with classical time-controls much more than I like online chess. Something about the personal feeling of being able to interact with your opponent gives it the human touch that chess so desperately requires. Lately, however, I have begun transitioning into the world of online chess and I have been gradually exploring faster time-controls that have been popular. I figured that, since chess was experiencing large changes over the past year, I would need to change my style too, lest I be left behind as a relic of a past age.

I used to play mainly on chess.com and chesskids.com for the first 7 years I played chess, from 2010 to 2017. After the major changes to the website, however, I found myself drawn to the more simplistic and less money-hungry interface of Lichess, where I currently play. These days, I'm nowhere near as good as I used to be, and so, I have been in a process of change to learn the modern openings in contrast to the bookish gameplay I had earlier.
